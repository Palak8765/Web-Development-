About Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh, aptly known as the "Heart of Incredible India" due to its central location, is a diverse state blending rich history, vibrant culture, and abundant wildlife. It features three UNESCO World Heritage sites—the Khajuraho temples, Sanchi Stupa, and Bhimbetka Rock Shelters—along with major tiger reserves like Kanha and Bandhavgarh, earning it the title of "Tiger State of India".

Madhya Pradesh, the Incredible Heart of India, is a land where nature, wildlife, and history come together beautifully. It is known for its dense green forests, majestic Bengal tigers, peaceful rivers, and breathtaking landscapes. The state is home to famous wildlife sanctuaries like Kanha and Bandhavgarh, along with timeless heritage sites, ancient temples, and stunning monuments. From rich traditions and vibrant culture to calm natural beauty, Madhya Pradesh offers an unforgettable experience filled with wild wonders and soulful greenery.

Let's Explore Madhya Pradesh Tourism

1.Bhopal Tourism

Places to Visit in Bhopal:

1.Lake View Point

Lake View in Bhopal, also known as Upper Lake or Bhojtal, is a serene freshwater lake offering mesmerizing sunsets, peaceful walks, and recreational boating. Overlooking the city's skyline, it stands as one of Bhopal's most loved natural attractions and a symbol of tranquility and beauty.

Map:
2.Van Vihar National Park

Van Vihar National Park and Zoo is one of the most revered National Park and Zoo of Central India and is a shining beacon in the field of conservation. It is an amazing world of winged creatures, free ranging ungulates and captive carnivores in the midst of greenery. The park which is more popularly known as just “Van Vihar” is considered as a “must see” destination for tourists visiting Bhopal. The characteristic combination of rich wetland areas, meadows, pools of water at different places, rugged slopes, mixed bamboo vegetation along with grass covered plateau and areas of mixed plantations support the rich floral and faunal diversity in the area.

Map:
3.Bharat Bhavan

Bharat Bhavan in Bhopal is famous as a multi-art center and museum, a unique hub for promoting India's diverse creative and performing arts, including folk/tribal art (Roopankar), theatre (Rangmandal), poetry (Vagarth), classical/folk music (Anhad), and cinema (Chhavi). Designed by Charles Correa with cascading terraces overlooking a lake, it's known for fostering contemporary artistic expression, housing significant collections, and hosting major cultural festivals and workshops

2.Indore Tourism

Places to Visit in Indore:

1.Lal Bagh Palace

Lal Bagh Palace in Indore, built by the Holkar dynasty between the late 19th and early 20th centuries (completed 1926), is a grand Italian Renaissance Revival palace showcasing opulent European styles, featuring Italian marble, Belgian stained glass, and gates modeled after Buckingham Palace, serving as a museum to display the dynasty's luxurious lifestyle and eventually becoming a protected state monument.

Map:
2.Kanch Mandir

Kanch Mandir, literally meaning Glass Temple, is a famous Jain temple located in Indore, built by Sir Seth Hukumchand Jain. Its construction began around 1903. Externally, the temple is built in the form of a medieval mansion with canopied balcony and shikhara.

3.Patalpani Waterfall

Patalpani is famous for its breathtaking, 300-foot waterfall near Indore, Madhya Pradesh, especially vibrant in monsoon, popular for picnics, trekking, and a scenic heritage train journey, with its name stemming from the legend that its deep pool reaches the underworld ("Patal"). It's a lush, hilly spot known for stunning landscapes, but caution is advised during heavy rains due to flash flood risks, as noted by past incidents, leading to improved safety measures like bridges.

3.Gwalior Tourism

Places to Visit in Gwalior:

1.Gwalior Fort

Standing on a steep mass of sandstone, Gwalior Fort dominates the city and is its most significant monument. It has been the scene of momentous events, imprisonment, battles and jauhars . A steep road winds upwards to the fort, flanked by statues of the Jain tirthankaras, carved into the rock face.

Map:
2.Man Mandir

Located at the North-east end of the fort, Man mandir palace was built between 1486 and 1516 by the Tomar ruler, Man Singh Tomar. The palace couldn't survive with time, however the remains of the palace still showcases the beautiful carvings and designs of that era. The palace has two open courts in between with apartments on two levels. There are underground prison cells as well, built later by Mughals.

Map:
3.Sun Temple

It was built in 1988 by the famous industrialist G.D. Birla. The temple is a replica of the famous Sun Temple of Konark in Orissa and is dedicated to the Sun God. The temple is known for its exquisite architecture, serene environment, and the beautiful gardens that surround it.
